The Artist

My Beginning

I'm Angela, the artist behind Angela's Glass Studio. I've always been drawn to color and light. But I didn't grow up thinking I was "an artist." That wasn't part of the plan. It wasn't something I believed about myself.

But I never forgot a glass project I made in a school art class. Something about it stuck with me in a quiet, persistent way. Years later, when I was a stay-at-home mom looking for something creative to do, that memory resurfaced… and I decided to try it again.

That one decision turned into everything.

I'm largely self-taught, learning through trial and error and the early days of online glass forums. I made plenty of mistakes, broke things, overfired things, underfired things... and learned from every single one. Over time, I built my skills across stained glass, glass fusing, and more dimensional, sculptural work.

Over time, glass stopped feeling like a hobby and started feeling like a language I understood.

After a move, I went about five years without a glass studio. That stretch pushed me somewhere unexpected. I started drawing and painting. Mixed media, acrylic, watercolor, anything I could get my hands on. It was the first time I truly saw myself as an artist, not just someone making things.

Even now, that time shows up in my glasswork. I think in layers, in movement, in color relationships the same way I would with paint. Glass became less about assembling pieces and more about composing something alive.

Where the Work Happens

The Studio

My studio is where experimentation lives. It's a space built around possibility. Shelves of glass in every color and texture, tools worn smooth from years of use, and the particular kind of quiet that settles in when you're deep in a piece.

Every work begins here, whether it's a small accent piece or a large architectural commission. I work across multiple techniques: stained glass, glass fusing, and more expressive dimensional work. The studio holds them all.

There's something about the light in a glass studio that never gets ordinary. The way it moves through color, shifts across the day, changes with the seasons. It's a reminder of why I fell in love with this medium in the first place. And why I keep showing up.

For me, the studio isn't just where I make things. It's where I think, where I solve problems, and where I stay curious. Every piece that leaves here carries a little of that with it.

A Defining Chapter

Study with Narcissus Quagliata

A major turning point in my work came from studying with Narcissus Quagliata, a world-renowned artist known for pushing the boundaries of what glass can be and say. That experience expanded everything for me. Not just in technique, but in how I think about the medium entirely.

Through his teaching, I explored new approaches to creating movement, depth, and texture. Working with frit, reactive glass, and pre-formed elements in ways I hadn't before. His approach to glass is expressive and fearless, and it gave me permission to loosen up, trust the process, and let the work breathe.

But beyond technique, it was the conversations that left the most lasting mark. The history, the connections, the deeper story of glass as a medium. Being introduced to other artists, hearing how they think, understanding the lineage of this craft shifted my perspective completely.

I don't just see glass as material anymore. I see it as light, as motion, as something constantly changing depending on how it's experienced.

More curious now than when I started.

Today, my work blends structure and spontaneity. Some pieces are clean and graphic. Others are organic and expressive. All of them are shaped by years of experimentation, curiosity, and a willingness to keep learning. I didn't start out believing I was an artist — but somewhere along the way, through glass, painting, mistakes, broken pieces, and persistence, I became one.
